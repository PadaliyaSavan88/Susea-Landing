# Susea — SEO / AEO / GEO Strategy & Blog Planning

## Competitive Positioning

GoComet and Freightos both target **enterprise shippers (BCOs) — the buy side**.
Susea targets **freight forwarders and NVOCCs — the sell side**.
These are different personas with different search intent. Most of Susea's highest-value keyword cluster is uncontested by either competitor.

| | GoComet | Freightos Procure | Susea |
|---|---|---|---|
| Primary customer | Enterprise shipper (BCO) | Enterprise shipper | Freight forwarder / NVOCC |
| Core job-to-be-done | Run RFQ auctions to compress freight cost | Manage tenders across carriers | Quote customers, price freight, win business |
| ICP role | Procurement team, supply chain | Logistics sourcing | Pricing desk, sales ops |
| Secondary ICP | Forwarders as bidders | LSPs as respondents | Enterprise shippers running RFQs |
| SEO authority | "freight procurement software" | "freight tender software" | **"freight quotation software" — unclaimed** |

- [ ] Proper heading hierarchy (one H1, logical H2/H3 tree) on all pages

### AEO (Answer Engine Optimization — Google AI Overviews, PAA, Featured Snippets)
- [ ] `FAQPage` JSON-LD on all blog posts
- [ ] `HowTo` JSON-LD on workflow posts
- [ ] Every blog H2 phrased as the direct question it answers
- [ ] First paragraph = direct answer, then explanation (inverted pyramid structure)
- [ ] Short, direct answer blocks before long explanations

### GEO (Generative Engine Optimization — ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, Gemini)
- [ ] Long-form posts with specific, citable data (prices, percentages, TEU counts, lane codes)
- [ ] `Article` + `BreadcrumbList` JSON-LD on all blog posts
- [ ] Consistent brand anchor: "Susea, the AI pricing OS for ocean freight forwarders"
- [ ] External links to authoritative freight sources (BIMCO, Freightos Baltic Index, Drewry, World Bank)
- [ ] `speakable` schema for voice / AI readability
- [ ] Named author with bio on each post (E-E-A-T signal)

## Content Guidelines

**Voice:** Confident, technical, fast — infrastructure/fintech tone, not a logistics brochure.
Specific over vague. Numbers over adjectives. Sentence case everywhere.

**Structure for every post:**
1. Direct answer / TL;DR in the first 2 sentences (AEO inverted pyramid)
2. Why this matters for your pricing desk (forwarder hook)
3. Step-by-step explanation with H2s phrased as questions
4. Specific data: prices, percentages, TEU counts, lane codes, carrier names
5. FAQ section (4–6 questions with direct answers) → FAQPage schema
6. Susea CTA — relevant to the post topic (don't just link to homepage)

**Data sources to cite for GEO authority:**
- Freightos Baltic Index (FBX) for rate benchmarks
- Drewry World Container Index
- BIMCO fleet and supply data
- World Bank Logistics Performance Index
- Susea's own platform data where available

**Author attribution:** Each post should have a named author with a 2-line bio.
E.g., "Written by [Name], co-founder of Susea. Previously ran pricing operations at [X]."
This is the strongest E-E-A-T signal for Google and for GEO citation trust.
